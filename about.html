I currently work at Google Research India as a Pre-Doctoral Researcher in the Advertising Sciences team, mentored by Dr. Aravindan Raghuveer, Dr. Karthikeyan Shanmugam and Dr. Rishi Saket. My work focuses on developing fair algorithms, especially under distribution shifts and developing scalable models for learning from aggregate data and privacy afforded by such aggregations.

I've graduated from IIT Hyderabad with a Bachelor of Technology with HONORS in 2022.

I have worked at Adobe Big Data Experience Lab as a Research Intern for the summer of 2021 and as a Machine Learning Intern at Japan Infra Waymark Corp. in the summer of 2020.

I've also been a member of Machine Learning and Vision Lab Group @ IIT Hyderabad headed by Dr. Vineeth N Balasubramanian. I've worked on applications of Influence Functions and the extensions to interpretability and explainability of models. I explored the robustness domain, and read and presented work from various domains across the AI space, especially Computer Vision. I have dabbled in a multitude of Computer Science Research Areas and have had the pleasure to work with Dr. Rogers Mathew for my Honors Thesis on Studying and investigating special cases union‑closed families to obtain insight about the union‑closed conjecture and studying and obtaining bounds sizes of families possessing properties like L‑intersection closedness, with Prof. M.V. Panduranga Rao on experimenting with different allocation strategies to mitigate the effects of noise on the performance of a Quantum Algorithm, and with Dr. Karteek Sreenivasaiah studying and obtaining complexity bounds for algorithms obtaining Nash Equilibrium for games.
